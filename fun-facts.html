Interesting Details About Henry David Thoreau

Fun Facts

Some fun facts and interesting quotes.

  • Henry David Thoreau was born David Henry Thoreau. He changed his name after college for seemingly no other reason than to reaffirm his identity.
  • While camping with his friend, Thoreau accidentally started a forest fire that burned 300 acres of land around Concord in 1844.
  • Thoreau collected many plant specimens for Harvard's botany library and the Boston Natural History Society.
  • He was inspired by Eastern literature and philosophy, for example ancient Hinduism and Confucius writings.
  • Thoreau's friend group consisted of many notable figures such as: Ralph Waldo Emerson, famous writer and philosopher; Nathaniel Hawthorne, famous novelist; The Alcott Family, including Bronson Alcott, education reformer, and Louisa May Alcott, author of Little Women.
  • The school the Thoreaus ran was known for its different approach. They frequently took students on field trips in nature, showed them how to make a boat, and taught applied mathematics with surveying equipment.
  • His cabin on Walden pond consisted of a bed, a table, a desk, and three chairs.
  • He once squeezed over 25 people in his small cabin.
  • Henry's younger sister Sophia compiled and edited his journals and letters after his death.
  • His final words were "Now comes good sailing."
