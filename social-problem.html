The Social Problem

Thoreau's Take on Modern Society and the Government

Thoreau's Critique of Society

While Henry David Thoreau was a member of the Transcendentalist movement and, by extension, for equal rights regardless of race or gender, he did not tackle these problems directly. He more so critiqued people's personal philosophies and contemplated how to live life to its fullest.

Transcendentalist Beliefs

Transcendentalism is a comparatively loosely based philosophy centered in the belief in the innate goodness of humanity and a subscription to high moral standing and an idealistic way of living. Due to their belief in the goodness of humanity, they were concerned with the popular abolitionist movement, among other humanitarian causes. Thoreau, in particular, emphasized the importance of individual integrity and the choice to "live deliberately."

Civil Disobedience

After spending a night in jail for refusing to pay his poll tax, a fee required to vote, Thoreau wrote "Civil Disobedience," perhaps his most famous essay, in which he argues that individuals have a moral duty to resist unjust laws and other actions by the government. He believed that people should hold their conscience over state policy, even if it means facing punishment. His refusing to pay his poll tax was a protest against the Mexican-American War.

The Abolitionist Movement

Thoreau was also good friends with the famous abolitionist John Brown. Brown organized the Harpers Ferry raid in Virginia, 1859, an attempt to start a slave rebellion. When the revolt failed and Brown was charged with treason, Thoreau spoke out fiercely. Although he opposed war in theory and practice, he welcomed the civil war as a way to bring about the end of slavery.

Preservation of Nature

Like many Transcendentalists, Thoreau found extreme power in nature. He believed that through introspective experiences with nature, people could find spiritual fulfillment. He was a champion for the preservation of nature and lamented the growing separation between people and the natural world.
