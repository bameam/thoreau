Bibliography

Essential Works by and About Henry David Thoreau

Works by Thoreau

Major Published Works

  • Walden; or, Life in the Woods (1854) - Thoreau's masterpiece describing his two-year experiment in simple living at Walden Pond. A seminal work in American literature exploring self-reliance, nature, and resistance to materialism.
  • Civil Disobedience (1849) - An essay on the right and duty of individuals to refuse allegiance to an unjust state. Profoundly influenced Gandhi, Martin Luther King Jr., and other civil rights leaders.
  • A Week on the Concord and Merrimack Rivers (1849) - A narrative of Thoreau's river journey with his brother, combining travel narrative with philosophical reflection.
  • The Maine Woods (1864, posthumous) - A collection of essays describing Thoreau's three trips to the Maine wilderness, showcasing his keen observations of nature.
  • Cape Cod (1865, posthumous) - Travel narrative and philosophical reflections from Thoreau's journeys along Cape Cod.
  • Excursions (1863) - A collection of essays and sketches published posthumously, featuring "Walking" and "Wild Apples."
  • The Correspondence of Henry David Thoreau - Letters revealing his relationships, thoughts, and correspondence with Ralph Waldo Emerson and others.

Selected Essays and Writings

  • Walking - Essay celebrating the act of walking as a spiritual and philosophical practice.
  • Slavery in Massachusetts - Response to the Fugitive Slave Act, expressing Thoreau's abolitionist views.
  • A Plea for Captain John Brown - Speech defending the abolitionist martyr John Brown.
  • Resistance to Civil Government - Alternative title for Civil Disobedience.
  • The Succession of Forest Trees - Scientific essay on natural forest regeneration.
  • Wild Apples - Essay about native apple trees and nature.

Critical and Biographical Studies

  • The Days of Henry Thoreau by Walter Harding - Comprehensive biography of Thoreau's life.
  • Thoreau: A Life of the Mind by Robert D. Richardson Jr. - Intellectual biography focusing on Thoreau's philosophical development.
  • Henry David Thoreau: A Life by Laura Dassow Walls - Modern biography integrating new scholarship.
  • The Thoreau You Don't Know by Robert D. Richardson Jr. - Essays exploring lesser-known aspects of Thoreau.
  • Thoreau and the Whale by Timothy Sweet - Studies Thoreau's engagement with nature and maritime culture.
  • Becoming Thoreau by David S. Reynolds - Examines how Thoreau developed his distinctive voice and philosophy.
  • Reading Thoreau's Journal - Scholarly analysis of Thoreau's personal journals and their significance.

Collections and Reference Works

  • The Portable Thoreau - Essential selections of Thoreau's major works in one volume.
  • The Princeton Edition of the Writings of Henry D. Thoreau - The most comprehensive scholarly edition of all Thoreau's writings.
  • Thoreau: Collected Essays and Poems - Modern collection featuring his essays, poems, and journal excerpts.
  • The Annotated Walden by Henry David Thoreau (annotated by Bill McKibben) - Walden with helpful annotations and commentary.
  • Henry David Thoreau: A Documentary Volume - Part of the Dictionary of Literary Biography, containing biography and documents.

Influence and Legacy

  • Thoreau's Legacy in the American Environmental Movement - Various scholarly works examining his influence on conservation.
  • Thoreau and Gandhi by Jay Parini - Studies the connection between Thoreau and Indian independence.
  • Martin Luther King Jr.'s Engagement with Thoreau's Civil Disobedience - Scholarly analyses of influence on Civil Rights.
  • The Thoreau Encyclopedia - Comprehensive reference guide to Thoreau's life, works, and concepts.

Digital Resources

  • The Walden Woods Project - Organization preserving Thoreau's legacy with online resources and research.
  • Project Gutenberg - Henry David Thoreau - Free access to Thoreau's major texts.
  • Thoreau's Journal Online - Digital versions of Thoreau's personal journals.
  • The Thoreau Society - Resources, publications, and annual conferences dedicated to Thoreau scholarship.
